Sauvage Space – Host Guidelines

Event Bookings & On-Site Stewardship · Internal reference for approved hosts

1. Purpose

This document exists to support hosts in delivering well-run, legally compliant, and context-aware events at Sauvage Space. The goal is to balance hospitality, neighbourhood respect, and creative expression while protecting the space, its residents, and its long-term viability.

2. Booking Workflow

  • Event confirmation must exist in the shared calendar and be backed by an invoice.
  • Deposit and written acceptance of the Sauvage Space Terms of Use must be received before access is granted.
  • Arrival, setup, event start, cleanup, and lockup times must be explicitly agreed in advance.
  • One named Host is responsible for the event and must be reachable by phone at all times.

3. Space Use

Hosts must clearly understand which zones are included in the booking (Entrance / Kitchen / Tasting Room / Upstairs). Maximum capacity, permitted activities, and alcohol service limitations (Meng-formula) must be respected at all times. Any use beyond the agreed zones or functions requires prior approval.

4. Preparation & Setup

  • Early access is only permitted if agreed in writing.
  • Use of resident equipment (including Frogs BV kitchen infrastructure) is not permitted without explicit approval.
  • Tableware, glassware, and furniture layouts must be agreed in advance and restored after the event.

5. During the Event

  • Sound levels must remain respectful of neighbours; quiet hours begin at 23:00 on weekdays and 00:00 on weekends.
  • Guest count must not exceed agreed limits (approx. 25 seated / 30 standing unless otherwise approved).
  • Alcohol may only be served during private events and in compliance with the Meng-formula licence.
  • Fire exits must remain unobstructed at all times; no smoking is permitted indoors.

6. Cleaning & Closing

  • The space must be returned to its original condition: trash removed, dishwasher run, surfaces cleaned, lights off.
  • Hosts are responsible for final lockup, alarm setting, and door checks.
  • Deposit deductions may apply for excessive cleaning, damage, pest risk (€100 minimum), or overtime use.

7. Communication & Emergencies

  • Hosts must have current contact details for Selection Sauvage BV and resident operators where relevant.
  • Any incident, damage, or neighbour complaint must be reported promptly.
  • In case of safety concerns, prioritise guests' wellbeing and secure the space.

8. Hosting Style

Sauvage events favour a considered, calm atmosphere: attentive hosting, thoughtful pacing, and respect for the space. The role of the Host is closer to stewardship than event production.

Appendix A — Closing Checklist

To be completed by the Host before leaving the premises
Space
  • All guests have exited the building
  • Furniture returned to agreed layout
  • Floors reasonably clean; spills addressed
  • Fire exits clear and unobstructed
Kitchen (if used)
  • Dishwasher run (or running)
  • All surfaces wiped and sanitized
  • Stoves / ovens off
  • Refrigerator and freezer doors fully closed
Waste
  • All trash removed from interior bins
  • Glass disposed of correctly
  • No food or perishables left overnight
Tech & Utilities
  • Music and AV equipment powered down
  • Lights turned off
  • Windows closed
Security & Lockup
  • Doors locked
  • Alarm set (if applicable)
  • Keys returned or secured as agreed
Final Check
  • No damage or issues to report
  • Any incidents documented and communicated
